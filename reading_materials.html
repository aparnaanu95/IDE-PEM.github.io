Module 1: Project Formulation

A project is a temporary activity undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. Projects are characterized by a defined objective, limited duration, and specific resource requirements. Effective project planning and management are essential to ensure that the objectives are achieved within the given constraints of time, cost, and quality.

Project formulation involves identifying investment opportunities and developing them into detailed project proposals. The process includes defining project objectives, estimating resource requirements, preparing technical designs, and evaluating financial feasibility.

Capital Investment

Capital investment refers to the allocation of funds for acquiring long-term assets such as land, buildings, machinery, and infrastructure. These investments are made with the expectation of generating economic benefits over an extended period.

Because capital investments involve large financial commitments and long-term implications, proper evaluation techniques such as cost–benefit analysis and financial appraisal are required before implementation.

Module 2: Feasibility Studies

A feasibility study is an analysis conducted to determine whether a proposed project is technically feasible, financially viable, and economically justified. It helps decision makers assess the practicality of the project before committing resources.

Feasibility studies generally examine technical requirements, market demand, financial returns, environmental considerations, and potential risks associated with the project.

Importance of Feasibility Study

  • Helps determine whether the project is achievable.
  • Identifies potential risks and uncertainties.
  • Supports effective financial planning.
  • Assists in selecting the best alternative project.
  • Improves decision making before large investments are made.
